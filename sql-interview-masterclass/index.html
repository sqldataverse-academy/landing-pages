Repeat Purchase Customers

You are working as a data analyst for an e-commerce company. The business team wants to identify customers who show strong repeat purchase behavior.

A customer is considered a repeat purchase customer if they placed at least 2 delivered orders within any 7-day period. This analysis helps identify highly engaged customers suitable for loyalty campaigns, retention programs, or personalized offers.

Business context: The marketing team needs this to launch a repeat-buyer loyalty program. Your query is the foundation of that campaign.

Table: orders

ColumnTypeDescription
order_idINTUnique identifier of the order
customer_idINTUnique identifier of the customer
order_dateDATEDate on which the order was placed
order_statusVARCHARDelivered / Cancelled / Returned / Failed
order_amountDECIMALOrder amount in INR

📌 Only order_status = 'Delivered' orders are relevant. Cancelled, returned, and failed orders must be excluded before any logic is applied.

Expected output — customers who had 2+ delivered orders within any 7-day window:

customer_idfirst_order_datesecond_order_datedays_between_orders
12024-01-012024-01-065
32024-03-052024-03-116
42024-04-102024-04-177
52024-05-042024-05-095
Why Customer 2 doesn't qualify: Their delivered orders are 11 days apart — outside the 7-day window.
Why Customer 3 qualifies: Cancelled order ignored — delivered orders are 6 days apart ✓
Exactly 7 days counts as valid — Customer 4 qualifies with exactly 7 days between orders.

SQL Server solution — step-by-step approach:

-- Step 1: Filter to only Delivered orders
WITH delivered_orders AS (
    SELECT
        order_id, customer_id,
        order_date, order_amount
    FROM orders
    WHERE order_status = 'Delivered'
),

-- Step 2: Self-join to find pairs within 7 days
qualifying_pairs AS (
    SELECT
        o1.customer_id,
        o1.order_date AS first_order_date,
        o2.order_date AS second_order_date,
        DATEDIFF(DAY, o1.order_date, o2.order_date) AS days_between_orders,
        -- Rank pairs to get the earliest qualifying pair
        ROW_NUMBER() OVER (
            PARTITION BY o1.customer_id
            ORDER BY o1.order_date, o2.order_date
        ) AS rn
    FROM delivered_orders o1
    INNER JOIN delivered_orders o2
        ON  o1.customer_id = o2.customer_id
        AND o1.order_date < o2.order_date
        AND DATEDIFF(DAY, o1.order_date, o2.order_date) <= 7
)

-- Step 3: Return earliest qualifying pair per customer
SELECT
    customer_id, first_order_date,
    second_order_date, days_between_orders
FROM qualifying_pairs
WHERE rn = 1
ORDER BY customer_id;
⚠️ This is what most candidates get wrong
This problem looks like a simple GROUP BY problem — but GROUP BY alone is not enough. The 7-day window requires comparing rows against each other, which means a self-join or window function approach.
The condition is NOT "customers with at least 2 delivered orders." The 2 orders must fall within any 7-day window — completely different logic.
Candidates forget to filter non-delivered orders first. If you include Cancelled/Returned/Failed orders before the logic, your window calculations are wrong.
Exactly 7 days is valid — using strict less-than (< 7) eliminates Customer 4 and gives a wrong answer.
If a customer has multiple qualifying pairs, return the earliest pair only — ROW_NUMBER() is the correct tool here.
